    THE STORY

    From Qeysarie bazaar To Canada

    The Grand Bazaar (in Persian: Bazar Bozorg, بازار بزرگ) is a historical market located in Isfahan, Iran, also known as the Qeysarriyeh Bazaar (in Persian: بازار قيصريه), Qeysarie bazaar or Soltani bazaar. The main commercial activities in the Qeysarie bazaar are carpet and kilim selling. The bazaar was one of the greatest and luxorious trading center in the safavid era for rugs for sale . It was built in 1620 on the northern side of Naqsh-e Jahan Square. It connects the Naqsh-e Jahan Square to the Atiq square and seljukid part of Isfahan.

    Mr. Gheisari, The Man Behind the rugs

    For centuries, Persian rugs have been an integral part of the Persian history and culture. The history of rugs has been handed down by fathers to their sons, keeping the legacy and tradition alive; a culmination of artistic & cultural brilliance. Mr. Gheisari, owner of Ziba Rug Gallery, took after his father’s footsteps who once had a charming antique & area rugs shop in 'The Grand Bazaar'. He grew up working alongside his father, growing a passion for the industry and the art of handmade Persian rugs that's embedded so deeply in his blood. For three generations, Ziba Rug Gallery has established a reputation for carrying the finest quality rugs in the market and is presently recognized as one of Toronto’s leading rug distributors & retailers in the northern GTA since 1989.
